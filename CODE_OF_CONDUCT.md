# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

This project is a professional space. We expect contributors to:

- **Be respectful.** Disagree with ideas, not with people.
- **Assume good faith.** Most misunderstandings are honest.
- **Be direct and kind.** Feedback helps; hostility does not.
- **Keep private things private.** Don't share others' private info without permission.

The following are not acceptable, regardless of intent:

- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment.
- Discrimination on the basis of race, gender, sexuality, disability, age, religion, nationality, or any other protected characteristic.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Sustained disruption of discussion or work.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.

## Scope

This code applies to all project spaces — the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any associated chat or mailing list — and to interactions between project members outside these spaces when the project's reputation is on the line.

## Reporting

If you see a violation or are on the receiving end of one, report it to the maintainer listed in `CODEOWNERS`. Use GitHub's private communication channels or the contact method in the repo README. Reports are kept confidential.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will review reports and respond with a proportionate action:

- Private warning.
- Public correction.
- Temporary ban from project spaces.
- Permanent removal from the project.

We'll explain our reasoning for any action taken. Appeals can be sent to the same channel; a different maintainer will review when possible.
